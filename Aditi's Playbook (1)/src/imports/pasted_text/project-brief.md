# PROJECT BRIEF

## Project Name

**The Guide I Wish I Had**

An interactive digital workspace where visitors can explore lessons, mistakes, frameworks, stories, and practical advice gathered from real experiences in Model United Nations, community building, event management, public speaking, leadership, and student life.

This is not a portfolio.

This is not a blog.

This is not a course.

It is a digital archive of experiences transformed into interactive guidance.

The goal is to make visitors feel as if they have discovered someone's personal operating system for navigating college, opportunities, communities, and growth.

---

# Core Concept

Imagine a visitor opening an old computer found in an abandoned indie game world.

The screen flickers to life.

A terminal welcomes them.

Folders exist everywhere.

Each folder contains lessons.

Each lesson contains stories.

Each story unlocks practical tools.

The experience should feel less like reading content and more like exploring a hidden digital workspace.

The visitor should constantly think:

"What happens if I click this?"

---

# Narrative Premise

The website belongs to a student who spent years figuring things out through trial and error.

MUN.

Communities.

Events.

Public speaking.

Leadership.

Networking.

Confidence.

Learning.

Instead of presenting achievements, the website documents discoveries.

The visitor is not being taught.

# Tone of Voice

The user has entered a thoughtfully designed digital workspace belonging to a curious, ambitious student.

The voice should feel:

* conversational but articulate
* creative but grounded
* playful but never childish
* confident but never boastful
* introspective without becoming emotional
* intelligent without sounding academic
* personal without oversharing

Avoid:

* corporate jargon
* LinkedIn buzzwords
* motivational language
* exaggerated self-promotion
* phrases like:

  * passionate
  * innovative
  * results-driven
  * visionary
  * dynamic
  * future leader

Every section should feel like a discovery rather than a sales pitch.

Assume the visitor is exploring a digital studio, workspace, or archive.

Use short deliberate sentences.

Prefer:

* observations over claims
* stories over adjectives
* specifics over generic praise

The reader should leave feeling:

"This person thinks differently."

Not:

"This person is trying to impress me."

Overall emotional effect:

* warm
* thoughtful
* slightly nostalgic
* quietly ambitious
